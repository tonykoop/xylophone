# Xylophone — Starter Songbook

The xylophone has wooden bars that produce a drier, more percussive tone than the glockenspiel.
Its resonators amplify each bar's natural pitch. It speaks quickly — ideal for dancing rhythms.

## Instrument range
- Beginner range used here: **F4–F6** (f to f'' in ABC notation)
- Full concert xylophone: F4–C8; soprano xylophones start at C5

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: stepwise, short range, simple rhythms
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: thirds and fifths, syncopation
- ★★★ — Advanced: runs, wide leaps, varied rhythms

## Mallet guidance
- Use medium-hard rubber mallets — softer mallets lose definition on wood bars.
- Xylophone resonates faster than glockenspiel; mallet rebound is critical.
- For dampening (staccato), briefly touch the bar edge after striking.
